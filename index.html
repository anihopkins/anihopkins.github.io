ABOUT


Ani Hopkins is an art historian, teacher, and computer scientist. She obtained a Bachelor of Science with dual minors in computer science and art history in 2019 from Tufts University. Her work focuses on the history of Armenian manuscript painting and its interactions with the architecture of its time.

In her professional life, Ani has been teaching computer science for three years, in subjects ranging from introductory C++ to web development, computational physics, machine structure, x86 Assembly programming, and software development in C.

Ani has worked for The Tufts Daily as a staff news writer, a columnist, a contributing opinion writer, and a website editor. She currently lives in Somerville, MA and teaches for Girls Who Code.

Outside of work, Ani likes comics, cooking, podcasts, and trains. She enjoys working on side projects of all natures, whether programming, writing, or expanding her visual arts practice.
